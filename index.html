Akan Naming Traditions

The Akan people of Ghana, Ivory Coast, and Togo have a unique tradition of naming their children based on the day of the week they were born and their birth order. These "day names" carry deep meanings related to the soul and character of the individual. Middle names often add variety, reflecting birth order, twin status, or ancestral connections. This naming tradition is not limited to the Akan people but is shared across West Africa and the African diaspora. Notable Ghanaians, such as Ghana's first president Kwame Nkrumah and former UN Secretary-General Kofi Annan, were named through this system. Variants of these names exist across different Akan subgroups and languages, often reflecting slight differences in pronunciation or affixes.
